Years of History

English Settlement Cemetery — also known as Harrison Cemetery — is located in Rome Township, Crawford County, Pennsylvania. It is the resting place of 141 men, women, and children, spanning from the late 1700s to the mid-20th century. Most were English immigrants or their descendants who came to northwestern Pennsylvania to build a new life.

The Project

This digital museum was created to preserve the stories of every person buried here. The cemetery has long been known to local historians and descendants but its stories have never been fully told in one place. This project changes that.

Every person on the timeline represents a life worth remembering. Some left extensive records. Others left only a name and a pair of dates carved in stone. Both deserve to be here.

How It Works

The People Timeline shows the lifespan of every known burial as a horizontal bar, sorted by death date. Bars are color-coded by family. Click any name to read their biography page. As research deepens, biography pages grow — new documents, photographs, and family connections are added over time.

The museum is designed to grow permanently. Every newly discovered document, every family photograph, every monument inscription adds to the record. Nothing is ever removed — only enriched.

Contributing

If you are a descendant, local historian, or anyone with information about a person buried here, please use the contribution form on their biography page. All submissions are reviewed by the museum curator before publication. You will be credited by name for any contribution that is used.
